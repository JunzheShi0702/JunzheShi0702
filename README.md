## About me

I'm an undergraduate student at Johns Hopkins University pursuing Computer Science and Applied Mathematics & Statistics. My work sits at the intersection of AI systems, full-stack engineering, and research workflows.

Currently, I am a Research Assistant in the Data Science for Psychiatry Lab, where I review and synthesize medical and clinical-AI literature to support a REACH-based EHR cohort study on thyroid hormone prescribing in psychiatric settings.

Before Hopkins, I worked on research projects in perovskite solar cells, subatomic physics simulation, and exoplanet light-curve modeling. Those projects shaped how I approach open-ended technical problems: start from messy evidence, build the right tools, and turn analysis into something reproducible.

## Selected work

### Atlas — AI-Assisted Course Search & Schedule Planning
Built a JHU course-search and schedule-planning platform with a full-stack team. Implemented personalized schedule-building workflows across a React frontend and Node.js/Express backend, connecting user preferences, course data, and LLM-assisted advising. Integrated PostgreSQL, pgvector, and OpenAI-powered tooling for course discovery and recommendations.

### PDR AI / LaunchStack — AI-Assisted Document Revision
Built a multi-step document rewrite workflow in Next.js and TypeScript, including file import, preview and diff review, regeneration, and stepwise acceptance of AI edits. Added rewrite-state persistence and integrated DOCX redlining into a legal editor through a validated API route and tests.

### ReferMe — Full-Stack Referral Platform
Developed a full-stack web application for users to seek and provide referrals, supporting registration, login, profile submission, and referral-provider profiles. Built frontend workflows with Angular and Angular Material and backend services with Node.js and Express.

### Research Projects
- Perovskite solar cells: contributed to experimental analysis, equipment/testing workflow improvements, and manuscript preparation.
- Subatomic physics: used ROOT on Ubuntu Linux and Glauber Monte Carlo simulation to analyze Pb-Pb nucleus collisions.
- Exoplanet modeling: used Lightkurve, NumPy, and Matplotlib to analyze TESS luminosity curves and model planet-star ratios.
